Introduction

About Me

I am a Ph.D. student in the Department of Kinesiology at Michigan State University, specializing in the Psychosocial Aspects of Sport and Physical Activity under the guidance of Dr. Nicholas D. Myers. I am also pursuing a Sports Analytics Graduate Certificate.

The central mission of my research is to advance and apply psychometric methodology to solve critical measurement challenges in health research. My work focuses on psychometric methodology for physical activity questionnaires, including factor analysis with ordered categorical indicators (CFA/ESEM), measurement invariance, and simulation-based evaluation.

Prior to joining MSU, I earned a Master of Sport Science and dual Bachelor's degrees in Mathematics Education and Sport Science from Chungnam National University in South Korea.

Focus Areas

Research Interests

The central mission of my research is to advance and apply psychometric methodology to solve critical measurement challenges in health research.

Psychometrics & Methods

Factor analysis with ordered categorical indicators (CFA/ESEM), measurement invariance, simulation-based evaluation of factor enumeration and latent vs. sum scoring for defensible, interpretable scores.

CFAESEMMonte CarloSimulation

Self-Efficacy & Measurement

Developing reporting and estimator guidance that strengthens construct validity in applied health research, with focus on physical activity self-efficacy measurement.

Self-EfficacyConstruct ValidityPASE

Population Health Analytics

Applying advanced analytic methods to population-level health data, including NHANES datasets, to understand relationships between physical activity, sedentary behavior, and health outcomes.

NHANESEpidemiologyBig DataSports Analytics

Questionnaire Development

Developing and validating questionnaires in sport and exercise psychology, including emotional regulation measures for athletes and well-being action scales.

Scale DevelopmentValidationWell-being
