Introduction

About Me

I am a Ph.D. student in the Department of Kinesiology at Michigan State University, specializing in the Psychosocial Aspects of Sport and Physical Activity under the guidance of Dr. Nicholas D. Myers. I am also pursuing a Sports Analytics Graduate Certificate.

The central mission of my research is to advance and apply psychometric methodology to solve critical measurement challenges in health research. A complementary mission is to develop computational psychometrics and physical-activity intervention models that leverage artificial intelligence, including large language models (LLMs), to improve measurement quality and enable scalable, personalized behavior change support.

Prior to joining MSU, I earned a Master of Sport Science and dual Bachelor's degrees in Mathematics Education and Sport Science from Chungnam National University in South Korea.

Focus Areas

Research Interests

The central mission of my research is to advance and apply psychometric methodology to solve critical measurement challenges in health research. A complementary mission is to develop computational psychometrics and physical-activity intervention models that leverage AI, including large language models, to improve measurement quality and enable scalable, personalized behavior change support.

Physical Activity Interventions

Developing scalable, personalized PA intervention models, including conversational agents powered by AI and LLMs, to support behavior change in diverse populations.

Behavior ChangeConversational AgentsAI

Self-Efficacy & Measurement

Developing reporting and estimator guidance that strengthens construct validity in applied health research, with focus on physical activity self-efficacy measurement.

Self-EfficacyConstruct ValidityPASE

Structural Equation Modeling

Factor analysis with ordered categorical indicators (CFA/ESEM), measurement invariance, and simulation-based evaluation of factor enumeration and latent vs. sum scoring for defensible, interpretable scores.

CFAESEMMeasurement Invariance

Population Health Analytics

Applying advanced analytic methods to population-level health data, including NHANES datasets, to understand relationships between physical activity, sedentary behavior, and health outcomes.

NHANESEpidemiologyBig Data

Monte Carlo Simulation

Designing and conducting simulation studies to evaluate the performance of psychometric methods under varied conditions, informing best practices for applied researchers.

SimulationFactor EnumerationLatent Scoring

LLM-Based Personas & Computational Psychometrics

Incorporating AI—especially LLM-based persona generation and computational psychometrics—to stress-test measurement models and support the development of scalable, personalized PA intervention models.

LLMPersona GenerationAI Psychometrics
